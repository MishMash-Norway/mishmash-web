# WP4 activities

### Interactive AI-driven reading experiences
Aims to develop a prototype that transforms books into interactive, AI-driven narrative systems, allowing readers (primarily children and adolescents) to converse with characters, co-author stories, and create multimedia content within the book's story world.  
**People involved:** Sashi Komandur (INN - lead), Bente Aasheim (Kulturtanken), Eirik Sørbø (UiA), and Jason Nelson (UiB/Centre for Digital Narrative).  
**Contact:** [Sashi Komandur](mailto:sashi.komandur@inn.no)

### AI Integration in Film and TV Education
This idea for a project within Mishmash aims both to investigate the adoption of AI tools among students and faculty within Norwegian Film and TV programs, pilot-testing at the University of Stavanger, and to evaluate and implement ethically grounded AI workflows into the traditional curriculum. The aim is to continue the work in the autumn with a larger curriculum project with other institutions in the Mishmash network.  
**People involved:** Sigmund Trageton (UiS - lead), Clemens Cavallin (NLA), Lars Nyre (UiB).  
**Contact:** [Sigmund Trageton](mailto:sigmund.trageton@uis.no)

### KI i kunstnerisk utdanning og praksis
A workshop-based project exploring how teachers and students at the Oslo National Academy of the Arts (KHiO) use generative AI in artistic development work, framed by activity theory and co-creation research. 
**People involved:** Tiril Schrøder (KHiO - lead), Øystein Stene (KHiO), Trine Wester (KHiO), Gro Skåland (Kristiania).  
**Contact:** [Tiril Schrøder](mailto:tiriSchr@khio.no)

### Scoping Review of Creative AI in Teaching and Learning
Conducts a systematic scoping review of existing research on Creative AI in artistic and educational contexts, to provide a knowledge foundation for all WP4 work and support external partners needing guidance. 
**People involved:** Sidsel Karlsen (NMH - lead), Eirik Sørbø (UiA), Fredrik Graver (INN), Felicity Baker (NMH), Øystein Olav Skaar (INN), Zacharias Andreadakis (HVL), Jens Knigge (NORD), Bjørn Tallak Bakken (INN).   
**Contact:** [Sidsel Karlsen](mailto:sidsel.karlsen@nmh.no)

### Teacher Resources for Critical AI Literacy 
This project will function as an early pilot for developing freely available, practically oriented resources helping primary and secondary school teachers integrate generative AI in creative subjects, through a structured series of workshops co-designed with teachers and educational organizations.  
**Peaople involved:** Eirik Sørbø (UiA - lead), Sidsel Karlsen (NMH), Fredrik Graver (INN), Heidi Partti (UniArts), Tine Grieg Viig (HVL), Matthew Landers (HVL).  
**Contact:** [Eirik Sørbø](mailto:eirik.sorbo@uia.no)
