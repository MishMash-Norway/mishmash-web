AI Integration in Film and TV Education

This idea for a project within Mishmash aims both to investigate the adoption of AI tools among students and faculty within Norwegian Film and TV programs, pilot-testing at the University of Stavanger, and to evaluate and implement ethically grounded AI workflows into the traditional curriculum. The aim is to continue the work in the autumn with a larger curriculum project with other institutions in the Mishmash network.
People involved: Sigmund Trageton (UiS - lead), Clemens Cavallin (NLA), Lars Nyre (UiB).
Contact: Sigmund Trageton

KI i kunstnerisk utdanning og praksis

“Creating with AI: An Introduction to AI in Artistic Education and Practice” is a collaborative project between arts educators and pedagogical researchers. Rooted in workshop-based learning traditions, the project emphasizes hands-on experimentation, play, and collaborative exploration as core methods for engaging with AI tools. Inspired by artistic and workshop practices, it champions the idea of “thinking with the hands”—learning through doing, exploring, and reflecting together.
The project looks at generative AI tools used in artistic development work (KUF) and individual artistic projects, balancing practical application with critical reflection. One goal is to find a form that can be used regularly to let teachers and students learn and update their knowledge together in a very fast moving field, to develop a pedagogical model where teachers and students co-explore AI as equals, fostering a dynamic learning environment shaped by curiosity, cooperation, discussions, shared experiences, and mutual growth.
The project centers on a 4-day workshop (week 45, autumn 2026) for students and faculty at the Oslo National Academy of the Arts (KHiO) and Kristiania University College, with open participation for other MishMash partners if capacity allows. The workshop will:
• Introduce and test AI tools for text, visuals, 3D, sound, and music production. • Have lectures and facilitate discussions on ethics, aesthetics, and guidelines for AI use in artistic practice.
• Examine how collaborative, workshop-based environments enhance both teaching and artistic practice/research in higher education.
• Explore makerspace learning, integrating experimental, hands-on AI tool use into arts education, art making and artistic research, and grow critical thinking, and technological literacy.
• Document ethical dilemmas, privacy concerns, and discuss guidelines for AI in artistic education and research, supporting their implementation in pedagogical practice. The workshop’s documentation will serve as a foundation for further artistic and pedagogical research and projects within MishMash, contributing to discussions about how art schools can navigate rapid technological change while upholding integrity, creativity and ethical considerations.
The project is framed by Activity Theory and addresses MishMash research questions on integrating AI to strengthen education, creativity, and ethical awareness. A 3-day preparatory workshop for organizers in May 2026 will focus on testing tools and planning the main event. Workshopen er åpen for: Studenter og fagansatte fra KHiO, Kristiania og andre MishMash-partnere, avhengig av ledige plasser.
People involved: Tiril Schrøder (KHiO - lead), Øystein Stene (KHiO), Trine Wester (KHiO), Gro Skåland (Kristiania), Synne Tollerud Bull (Kristiania).
Contact: Tiril Schrøder

Scoping Review of Creative AI in Teaching and Learning

Conducts a systematic scoping review of existing research on Creative AI in artistic and educational contexts, to provide a knowledge foundation for all WP4 work and support external partners needing guidance. People involved: Sidsel Karlsen (NMH - lead), Eirik Sørbø (UiA), Fredrik Graver (INN), Øystein Olav Skaar (INN), Zacharias Andreadakis (HVL), Jens Knigge (NORD), Bjørn Tallak Bakken (INN).
Contact: Sidsel Karlsen

Teacher Resources for Critical AI Literacy

This project will function as an early pilot for developing freely available, practically oriented resources helping primary and secondary school teachers integrate generative AI in creative subjects, through a structured series of workshops co-designed with teachers and educational organizations.
Peaople involved: Eirik Sørbø (UiA - lead), Sidsel Karlsen (NMH), Fredrik Graver (INN), Heidi Partti (UniArts), Tine Grieg Viig (HVL), Matthew Landers (HVL).
Contact: Eirik Sørbø

Interactive AI-driven reading experiences

Aims to develop a prototype that transforms books into interactive, AI-driven narrative systems, allowing readers (primarily children and adolescents) to converse with characters, co-author stories, and create multimedia content within the book’s story world.
People involved: Sashi Komandur (INN - lead), Bente Aasheim (Kulturtanken), Eirik Sørbø (UiA), and Jason Nelson (UiB/Centre for Digital Narrative).
Contact: Sashi Komandur
