summary: 'Born in 1977 in Corinth, Greece, I am a visual artist based between Oslo
  and Corinth. My practice moves between moving image, installation, and research,
  and combines classical training in painting and material making with computational
  image systems. I work with both my hands and digital tools, treating media not only
  as instruments of production but also as ways of seeing and understanding the world.
  My work focuses on how images are not neutral representations, but systems that
  organise visibility, attention, and power. I am interested in how technical, social,
  and cultural infrastructures shape what can be seen, shared, and understood, and
  what is excluded or made invisible.


  I hold an MA in Fine Arts from Sandberg Instituut, Amsterdam (2010), a BA in Audio/Visual
  from Gerrit Rietveld Academie, Amsterdam (2008), and a BA in Fine Arts from the
  Athens School of Fine Arts (2004), including an Erasmus exchange at Kunsthochschule
  Weissensee, Berlin (2003).


  My professional experience includes working in Berlin in 2020 for a large social
  media company as a content moderator, training facial recognition systems. The experience
  informs my artistic practice and my interest in how images are classified, sorted,
  and made legible by machines and institutions.


  My practice is research-based and often collaborative. I work with moving image,
  installation, and site-based methods, combining digital processes with manual practices
  of editing, making, and material construction. My current research focuses on generative
  AI and archival film, especially early silent cinema and public domain footage.
  I am interested in how everyday life is shaped by image systems and intersecting
  technical, social, and cultural infrastructures. These systems do not simply show
  the world; they actively produce it, organise it, and limit how it can be perceived
  and understood. My work follows these operations at the level of editing, where
  meaning is constantly constructed, disrupted, and reconfigured.


  I have experience in teaching and workshop-based work in artistic and educational
  contexts.


  I am the founder and curator of Corinth Art Platform (CAP), a platform for contemporary
  art and public projects in Corinth, Greece. I also participate in collaborative
  research practices focused on art, technology, and public space.


  I am a member of the Norwegian Visual Artists (NBK) and the Young Artists’ Society
  (UKS).'
