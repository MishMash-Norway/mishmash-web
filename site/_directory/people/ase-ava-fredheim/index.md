Åse Áva Fredheim is a vocalist, songwriter, and producer, and a PhD research fellow at the Department of Popular Music, University of Agder. Her doctoral project *Sound Heritage* explores the intersection of musicology and Indigenous knowledge through both an artistic and academic approach, focusing on field recording practices, Sámi vocal traditions such as luohti/joik, and listening. She holds a master's degree from the University of Oslo.

Through MishMash seed funding she carries out a [research stay at the Centre for Sami Studies](/projects/sound-heritage-research-stay/) at UiT The Arctic University of Norway.
