## Summary

This project explores how creative technologies combining music, movement and interaction can contribute to wellbeing, engagement and participation among people living with dementia. It uses Muzziball — an interactive sensory ball combining music, movement and sensory stimulation, which has recently entered implementation through a four-year framework agreement with Stavanger municipality — as a case study for investigating human-centred approaches to technology development in healthcare. Activities include a workshop bringing together researchers, artists, healthcare professionals and technology developers from the MishMash network, analysis of experiences from existing pilot activities, exploration of AI-supported personalisation and adaptive interaction, and a roadmap for future collaborative research and funding applications.
