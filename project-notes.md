The following summaries cover the projects selected for the first round of MishMash seed funding. Each project explores the intersection of artificial intelligence and creative practice across seven defined work packages.

### **1. Trondheim Kreativ AI (TKAI)**
*   **Project Leader:** Viggo Tellefsen Wivestad
*   **Institution:** SINTEF Digital
*   **Other Participants:** Audun Kvitland Røstad (musician), Tor Martin Norvik, and Øystein Eugene Hermstad (Super Ponni)
*   **Summary:** TKAI is a meetup series based in Trondheim that brings together researchers, artists, and creative professionals to explore how AI is transforming media, design, and music. Through three open seminars in 2026, the project will focus on themes such as AI-generated music, deepfakes, and emerging creative workflows. The initiative provides a platform where MishMash researchers and external creative communities can network and discuss the ethical, legal, and economic implications of AI.

### **2. Archival Intelligence**
*   **Project Leader:** Budhaditya Chattopadhyay
*   **Institution:** Department of Contemporary Art, University of Bergen
*   **Other Participants:** Lydgalleriet (Bergen), Bergen Senter for Elektronisk Kunst (BEK), KMD University of Bergen
*   **Summary:** This "artivist" project aims to liberate institutional archives from colonial storage through participatory storytelling and the innovative use of machine learning. It manifests as a public exhibition at Lydgalleriet and a related two-day seminar titled “Archival Intelligence: Epistemic Shifts from Sound to Listening.” By examining the nexus between European archives and corporate AI training datasets, the project seeks to transform stagnant repositories into dynamic, liberated spaces for artistic performance and cultural heritage.

### **3. AI Integration in Film and TV Education**
*   **Project Leader:** Sigmund Trageton
*   **Institution:** University of Stavanger (UiS/IMS)
*   **Other Participants:** Clemens Cavallin (NLA), Lars Nyre (UiB), Screenstory (represented by Tommy Aase)
*   **Summary:** This project investigates the adoption of AI tools among students and faculty within Norwegian Film and TV programs to implement ethically grounded workflows into traditional curricula. The team uses innovation pedagogy to bridge the gap between frantic technological evolution and traditional university structures. Activities include a hands-on workshop where students test AI tools on active projects and a strategic R&D meeting to coordinate a larger curriculum project across the MishMash network.

### **4. RhyGlyph to RhyDiff to RhySeq**
*   **Project Leader:** Çağrı Erdem
*   **Institution:** Department of Informatics, University of Oslo (UiO)
*   **Other Participants:** Ivar Grydeland (NMH/UiA), Halldór Úlfarsson and Thor Magnusson (Intelligent Instruments Lab, University of Iceland)
*   **Summary:** This project advances ongoing rhythm interface research into a self-contained generative AI instrument prototype named "RhySeq." The development includes hardware design in Iceland and evaluation through music student workshops at UiA and NMH, focusing on relational steering and human-machine control dynamics. Designed for both music-making and rhythm analysis, the resulting prototype, open-source software, and trained models will be shared to support broader artistic and scientific research.

### **5. Intensifying Brain Pleasure Responses to Music**
*   **Project Leader:** Sascha Frühholz
*   **Institution:** Department of Psychology, University of Oslo (UiO)
*   **Other Participants:** Jonna Vuoskoski (UiO Department of Musicology)
*   **Summary:** This study utilizes real-time fMRI neuroimaging and neurofeedback at the University Hospital of Oslo to quantify brain signals in the neural pleasure centers of listeners. An AI algorithm instantaneously learns patterns in these neural signals to intelligently modify musical features—such as tempo, rhythm, and timbre—in real-time to enhance the listener's experience. The project investigates how AI-controlled musical creations can intensify human pleasure, replacing human performers with adaptive code.

### **6. AI-Assisted Film Production through Vita Vigeland**
*   **Project Leader:** Henning Birkeland
*   **Institution:** Department of Performing Arts, Film and Media, Kristiania University College
*   **Other Participants:** Ole Christian Solbakken (University of Inland Norway)
*   **Summary:** Using screenwriter Ole Christian Solbakken’s screenplay *Vita Vigeland* as a case study, this project examines how generative AI can be integrated into contemporary film preproduction. The research tests how AI tools can prototype visual dramaturgy, tone, and atmosphere for a biographical drama while reflecting on artistic control and authorships. The project contributes to Solbakken’s ongoing PhD research, documenting hybrid workflows that combine traditional film practice with emerging AI technologies.

### **7. Human–Machine Consciousness**
*   **Project Leader:** Victoria Johnson
*   **Institution:** Department of Musicology, University of Oslo (UiO)
*   **Other Participants:** Øyvind Brandsegg (NTNU)
*   **Summary:** This project develops a new artistic work for instrument, live electronics, and real-time AI, positioning the machine as a responsive co-performer rather than an autonomous creator. Through structured workshops in Trondheim and Oslo, the team explores consciousness, agency, and intentionality within human–machine musical systems. The work features a flexibly notated score to allow for adaptation by other instruments, producing both artistic performances and research on performer-centred AI design.

### **8. When Creativity Meets Criticality**
*   **Project Leader:** Bjørn Tallak Bakken
*   **Institution:** University of Inland Norway (INN)
*   **Other Participants:** Gerard Hodgkinson (University of Manchester), Magda Osman (University of Cambridge), Carsten Griwodz (MishMash WP7 Lead)
*   **Summary:** This project organizes an international academic workshop to discuss how creative AI can improve decision-making in volatile and complex (VUCA) environments. The workshop addresses the contribution of AI to societal safety and security, as well as the pitfalls of autonomous technology in crisis management. The resulting research agenda and conceptual paper will provide a foundation for empirical studies on AI usage in environments where improvisation and creativity are essential to solving global crises.

### **9. Scoping Review of Creative AI in Teaching and Learning**
*   **Project Leader:** Sidsel Karlsen
*   **Institution:** Norwegian Academy of Music (NMH)
*   **Other Participants:** Eirik Sørbø (UiA), Fredrik Graver (INN), Felicity Baker (University of Melbourne)
*   **Summary:** This project initiates a systematic scoping review of the artistic and pedagogical use of AI within formal, non-formal, and informal learning contexts. The work is designed to serve as a foundation for researchers across MishMash and help external partners provide AI literacy guidelines to teachers and school leaders. The project includes an online seminar and a physical workshop, culminating in a peer-reviewed scientific article that synthesizes current knowledge in the field.

### **10. Bingo: AI-Assisted Animation for Documentary Film**
*   **Project Leader:** Tonje Louise Finne
*   **Institution:** TV-School, University of Inland Norway (INN)
*   **Other Participants:** Henning Birkeland (Kristiania University College), Ole Christoffer Haga (INN)
*   **Summary:** The project "Bingo" explores the use of generative AI as a controllable tool for creating animations in a children’s documentary film. It focuses on developing prototype sequences to convey parts of the story that could not be captured on film, such as a mother cat's life in an outdoor colony. The research tests how AI tools can be constrained to maintain cultural specificity and artistic control, resulting in a public report on artist-centred workflows and reflections on bias and authenticity.

### **11. Evaluation of Cultural Bias and Workflow in AI Music Generation**
*   **Project Leader:** Shayan Dadman
*   **Institution:** Department of Computer Science and Computational Engineering, UiT Narvik
*   **Other Participants:** Andreas Bergsland (NTNU), Jon Marius Aareskjold-Drecker (UiO)
*   **Summary:** This project validates SCOPE (Situated Creative Operation and Process Evaluation), a framework that assesses AI music tools based on their effectiveness in supporting real creative workflows. Five semi-professional musicians will spend a day working with tools like Suno and Udio while researchers measure technical performance and creative control. The study aims to uncover hidden biases in these systems and provide open-source templates for practitioners to evaluate how AI tools fragment or support their creative practice.

### **12. “Tool for tools”**
*   **Project Leader:** Troels Linde
*   **Institution:** FTVS, University of Inland Norway (INN)
*   **Other Participants:** Irina Eidsvold-Tøien (BI), Kjetil Nordby (AHO), Zacharias Andreadakis (HVL), Oslo Kreativ AI (OKAI)
*   **Summary:** Submitted by the subgroup "Horizontal 257," this initiative develops a living AI Tools Catalogue to be hosted on the MishMash website. The project maps diverse AI tools for creative research—covering image, video, audio, text, and code—detailing their uses, required proficiency, and relevance to MishMash work packages. It launches through a public talk, a researcher seminar, and a hands-on workshop to facilitate shared infrastructure and knowledge across the entire 200+ researcher network.

### **13. KI i kunstnerisk utdanning og praksis**
*   **Project Leader:** Tiril Schrøder
*   **Institution:** Kunsthøgskolen i Oslo (KHiO)
*   **Other Participants:** Øystein Stene (KHiO), Trine Wester (KHiO), Gro Skåland (Kristiania University College)
*   **Summary:** This collaboration between artists and pedagogues explores how generative AI is used in artistic development and meaning-making. Centred on a four-day workshop, participants test AI tools for text, image, 3D, and sound through methods rooted in the workshop tradition. The project investigates how individual artistic practices can be strengthened by AI while documenting reflections on ethics and aesthetics to develop teaching models that can be regularly updated as the technology evolves.

### **14. Strategic Storyteller**
*   **Project Leader:** Terje Gaustad
*   **Institution:** Kristiania University College
*   **Other Participants:** Aedan Soellaart (INN), Lukas Erni (Independent consultant), Sashi Komandur (INN)
*   **Summary:** This project explores the value of artistic skills in AI workflows by investigating whether tailored, human-centric systems outperform standard Large Language Models (LLMs). The team is developing a "Logic Layer" for strategic storytelling that acts as a structural editor to counteract the Western-centric, conflict-driven biases often found in standard AI outputs. The project will result in a comparative analysis of narratives generated by specialized versus general tools, providing a proof of concept for more inclusive, artist-centred AI systems.

### **15. Copyright and Personality Rights in the AI Era**
*   **Project Leader:** Irina Eidsvold-Tøien
*   **Institution:** BI Norwegian Business School
*   **Other Participants:** Ole-Andreas Rognstad (University of Oslo)
*   **Summary:** This legal research investigates the risks generative AI poses to performers, specifically regarding the unauthorized use of recordings for model training and the creation of imitations mimicking a performer's voice or appearance. The study analyzes Nordic Supreme Court decisions to identify the structure and scope of protection for personal identity expressions. By comparing Nordic and European law with the American system, the project aims to determine if new regulations are necessary to sustainably protect performers' contributions in the digital age.

### **16. Musical Doppelgängers**
*   **Project Leader:** Peter Edwards
*   **Institution:** IMV, University of Oslo (UiO)
*   **Other Participants:** Ivar Grydeland (NMH), Anders Tveit (NOTAM), Christian Blom (NOTAM), Dániel Péter Biró (UiB), Heloisa Amaral (Ultima)
*   **Summary:** In collaboration with the Ultima festival, this project uses the Disklavier self-playing grand piano to explore the bridge between player piano systems and modern learning machines. Through performances, masterclasses, and lectures, researchers will trace how musical gesture and style can be replayed and transformed without the original player. The project examines how technical replay reshapes time, agency, and presence, often incorporating other technologies like fNIRS brain-scanning helmets to probe the uncanny nature of machine performance.

### **17. Environmental Sustainability in MishMash**
*   **Project Leader:** Lina Plataniti
*   **Institution:** NORSUS
*   **Other Participants:** Erik Johannes Husom (SINTEF), Sagar Sen (SINTEF Digital)
*   **Summary:** This project establishes a knowledge base and maps the competences of MishMash partners working on the environmental sustainability of AI systems. Through an in-person workshop, the team identifies synergies and set goals for evaluating the sustainability of AI and creativity within the consortium. The output includes a blog post documenting findings and ideas for future project proposals, ensuring that responsible and sustainable development is integrated into MishMash’s creative and cultural research.

### **18. AI as Environment**
*   **Project Leader:** Synne Tollerud Bull
*   **Institution:** Kristiania University College
*   **Other Participants:** Eivind Røssaak (National Library), Budhaditya Chattopadhyay (UiB), Stephanie von Spreter, Susanne Ø. Sæther
*   **Summary:** This initiative supports a research visit to the exhibition *AI and the Paradox of Agency* in Umeå to analyze AI as an epistemic and institutional environment in artistic production. The visit aims to strengthen collaboration between artistic and infrastructural perspectives within MishMash and initiate a dialogue with WASP–HS toward a future Horizon Europe proposal. The delegation explores how AI is mediated institutionally through exhibition design and knowledge infrastructures, resulting in a public report and a concept note for future international funding.

### **19. Movement-to-Music Hackathon**
*   **Project Leader:** Andreas Bergsland
*   **Institution:** NTNU
*   **Other Participants:** Robert Wechsler (MotionComposer GmbH)
*   **Summary:** This two-day hackathon at NTNU explores AI-based pose detection (such as Google’s MediaPipe) as a foundation for movement-to-music interaction in therapeutic and wellbeing settings. Researchers, artists, and students collaboratively prototype mapping strategies between body movement and musical parameters, evaluating how affordable markerless tracking compares to established computer vision techniques. The project produces an open-source code repository and documentation to support future large-scale funding applications for AI-assisted therapeutic interactions.

### **20. Teacher Resources for Critical AI Literacy**
*   **Project Leader:** Eirik Sørbø
*   **Institution:** University of Agder (UiA)
*   **Other Participants:** Heidi Partti (Uniarts Helsinki), Sidsel Karlsen (NMH), Fredrik Graver (INN), and Norwegian educational organizations (Musikk i skolen, etc.)
*   **Summary:** This project develops practice-oriented, scalable resources for primary and secondary teachers on the pedagogical use of generative AI in creative subjects. Through co-creation workshops with researchers and school teachers, the team translates theoretical insights on AI literacy into concrete professional development materials. The project establishes models for resource distribution across school networks, contributing to a broad uptake of AI literacy tools throughout the Norwegian school ecology.

### **21. Interactive AI-Driven Book Experiences**
*   **Project Leader:** Eirik Sørbø
*   **Institution:** University of Agder (UiA)
*   **Other Participants:** Bente Aasheim (Kulturtanken), Sashi Komandur (INN), Fredrik Graver (INN), Sidsel Karlsen (NMH), Jason Nelson (UiB)
*   **Summary:** This pilot project develops a prototype that transforms linear books into interactive, AI-driven narrative systems for children and adolescents. Readers can engage in virtual dialogues with authors and book characters or use AI to create their own stories and music based on the book's world. Developed through workshops and student programming at INN, the prototype aims to extend Interpretation and imagination in reading while exploring how interactive storytelling can function as a future form of learning.

### **22. TAO - Transductive Acoustic Organism**
*   **Project Leader:** Enrique Encinas
*   **Institution:** Oslo School of Architecture and Design (AHO)
*   **Other Participants:** Kjetil Nordby (AHO), Synne Tollerud Bull (Kristiania), Ida Jahr (INN)
*   **Summary:** TAO is a persistent AI research instrument designed to run continuously and accumulate context over months across multiple domains. It features two prototypes: "Open Ambient," an acoustic telepresence system for maritime operations, and "Warp," a performance instrument for navigating AI latent space as creative material. The project also includes a networking workshop at Ars Electronica to connect MishMash researchers and explore the boundary between analysis and confabulation in persistent AI systems.
